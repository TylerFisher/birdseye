Photographer Shares Her Full Take From Juarez, Mexico

By Team Trinidad | April 9, 2015

In 2010, there were over 3,000 murders in Juarez, Mexico. Most were related to warring gangs and drug cartels. Nightly news media published images of blood soaked sidewalks, detached limbs and overall chaos. War photographers moved into the city, as innocent families fled.

Once the violence slowed down, everyone turned away. Last year the city’s homicide rate fell below 500.

Today, many say the city is on the mend and people are returning to a semblance of peace. But walk around some neighborhoods, like Riberas del Bravo, and you’ll see a different story. We sent our photographer there to show us what life looks like fours years after the drug war erupted on their doorstep.
